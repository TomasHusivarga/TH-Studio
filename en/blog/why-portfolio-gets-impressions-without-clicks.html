A portfolio gets impressions but no clicks. What does that mean?

This is one of those things that looks worse than it is. Search Console shows impressions, clicks are still zero, and the first reaction is that SEO is broken. But when I checked the actual queries, the issue was different.

I checked the queries first, not just the graph

The graph can show that something is happening, but it does not explain why. So I did not look only at the number of impressions. I opened the actual queries where the portfolio was appearing.

That changed the whole picture. Most impressions were not coming from searches like website development, WordPress website development or WordPress edits. Google was mainly showing project pages for company names and client websites. That is a completely different search intent.

An impression is not an inquiry

An impression only means the page appeared in search results. It does not mean the person was looking for a service. If someone searches for a specific clinic, shop or company, they usually do not want to click the portfolio of the person who built the website.

So zero clicks does not automatically mean the page, design or offer is bad. It can simply mean the page is currently appearing for searches where people have no real reason to click.

What I changed

I did not treat this as a reason to rewrite the whole site. First, it made sense to clean up the things that help both Google and people understand what is a service page and what is just a work example.

  • Service links got more specific wording instead of generic “service detail” text.
  • Project pages started linking to related services, for example WordPress website development or WooCommerce store development.
  • I fixed one broken internal link that was pointing away from the right page.
  • The sitemap dates were updated for the pages that changed.

What I would watch next

After this kind of cleanup, I would not judge the site only by total impressions. I would watch whether queries like website development, WordPress website development, e-shop development, WooCommerce store or WordPress edits start appearing.

If those queries show up, it makes sense to tune titles, meta descriptions and page copy based on real data. If they do not show up at all, the issue is more likely authority and links from outside the site.

Short takeaway

Not every impression is a useful impression. First check the intent behind the query. Only then does it make sense to adjust titles, page text or add more content.
